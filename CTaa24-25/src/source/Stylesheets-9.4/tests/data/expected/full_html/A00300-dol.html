A. 3. Caption for image of Noah

Tablet: Mosaic floor.
Dialect: Mosaic lettering, in a panel representing Noah as he releases the dove from the Ark.
Alphabet: 0.09; lunate epsilon, omega.

Date: Sixth century A.D.

Findspot: Apollonia: East Church: on the floor of the south transept; found in 1920.
Original location: Findspot.
Current location: Apollonia Museum.

Bibliography: 9, whence SEG 18.760 (= PHI 324459); 3 and pl. LVII; p. 150, with plate, and discussion at 61, whence SEG 30.1782 (= PHI 324530); 57.1.
Editor: Joyce M. Reynolds
Text constituted from: Transcription (Reynolds).

Νῶε

Apparatus:

Translation:

Noah.

Commentary:

This is, so far, the only church mosaic in Cyrenaica to show a biblical scene. Rosenbaum discussed its depiction of Noah releasing from the ark, arguing that it is is rather old-fashioned for the time.
